https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/business-studies/social-responsibilities-of -a-business-and-its-need/ A business is not just a proﬁt-making entity; it is a vital part of the society in which it operates. It depends on society for essential resources such as labor, capital, technology, and raw materials, and it also relies on the community as its market for goods and services. This close connection means that a business cannot operate in isolation. Its actions, decisions, and operations have a direct impact on people, communities, and the environment. In simple terms, social responsibility means that a business should not focus solely on earning proﬁts. It should also ensure that its operations do not harm society or the environment and, wherever possible, contribute positively to social welfare and sustainable development. This perspective encourages businesses to take a long-term view of their role, recognizing that the well-being of the community and the environment is closely linked to their own continued growth and success. However, according to critics, social responsibility practices are the exact opposites of the major motive of a business's existence. Social responsibility in business is also known as Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR). Some of the companies strongly committed to CSR are IKEA, Bosch, Apple, Ben and Jerry's, Starbucks, Marc Jacobs, and Adidas.
